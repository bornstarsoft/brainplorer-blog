The Game Design and Implementation program at Capital Region BOCES stands out as a vital educational initiative that equips students with the technical and collaborative skills needed in todays booming gaming and tech industries. With nearly a decade of experience, this program not only teaches coding and game development but also fosters teamwork and creativity, preparing students for a variety of career paths beyond just game design.

Under the guidance of instructor Astra Wijaya, students like Michael Satterlee and Mikkail Warner are diving deep into the game development process, transforming traditional board games into engaging virtual experiences. This hands-on approach allows them to explore different roles within the industry, from level design to programming, giving them a comprehensive understanding of what it takes to succeed in the field.
