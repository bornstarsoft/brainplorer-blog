On April 9, 2001, Dr. Mario 64 was released, marking the end of an era for the Nintendo 64. This game encapsulated everything that made the N64 special: engaging multiplayer experiences, charming characters, and a refined take on classic gameplay mechanics. As the last major title developed by Nintendo for the console, it serves as a poignant reminder of the platform's legacy and its impact on gaming culture.

Dr. Mario 64 brought a fresh twist to the beloved puzzle genre, allowing up to four players to engage in competitive fun while also offering a robust single-player mode. The game may not have achieved blockbuster sales, but its emphasis on multiplayer camaraderie and classic Mario charm made it a fitting swan song for the N64. As we celebrate its 25th anniversary, we reflect on how Dr. Mario 64 symbolizes the transition from one gaming generation to the next.
