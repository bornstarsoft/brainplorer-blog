In a surprising turn of events, Nagoshi Studio, founded by the renowned Yakuza series creator Toshihiro Nagoshi, is reportedly on the brink of closure after publisher NetEase announced it would withdraw funding for their debut title, Gang of Dragon. This development comes just three months after the game was unveiled at The Game Awards, highlighting the precarious nature of funding in the gaming industry.

The decision by NetEase to cut off financial support stems from revelations that Gang of Dragon requires an additional 7 billion (approximately $44.4 million) to reach completion. Despite Nagoshi's efforts to secure alternative funding, the studio has yet to find a viable solution. The game's star, Ma Dong-seok, known for his roles in popular films like Train to Busan, adds to the project's high expectations, making this news even more disheartening for fans.

As the gaming community watches closely, one can't help but wonder: what does this mean for the future of innovative studios like Nagoshi's? Will they find the support needed to bring their creative visions to life, or will financial hurdles stifle new ideas in the industry?
